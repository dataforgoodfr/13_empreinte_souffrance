Where does the Welfare Footprint come from?
Researchers at the Welfare Footprint Institute developed the first scientific methodology to quantify animal suffering on farms. The egg Welfare Footprint is based on over 100 sources of suffering for hens, both physical (bone fractures, skin lesions, illnesses…) and psychological (restriction of natural behaviors, fear, hunger…).
The associations Anima and Data for Good communicate about the egg Welfare Footprint as part of the "L'heure des comptes" campaign. Its goal: inform consumers and hold supermarkets accountable for the suffering of caged hens.
What do the different types of pain represent?
Annoying

Mild discomfort, no impact on behavior. Comparable to an itch or slightly tight shoes.

Criteria: attention capacity preserved, no behavioral or physiological signs.

Hurtful

Persistent pain, altering behaviors without preventing them. Similar to a headache or chronic back pain.

Criteria: reduction in pleasurable activities, mild cognitive impairment, analgesic effectiveness.

Disabling

Constant pain, altering behaviors without preventing them. Similar to a headache or chronic back pain.

Criteria: reduction in pleasurable activities, mild cognitive impairment, analgesic effectiveness.

Excruciating

Constant pain, dominating all other priorities. Reduces activity, well-being, attention. Similar to migraine or a fracture.

Criteria: drastic activity change, non-responsiveness to environment, need for strong medication.
